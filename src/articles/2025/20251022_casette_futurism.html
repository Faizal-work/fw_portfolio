Casette Futurism: Does it speak to me? 19-October-2025 by Faizal.Work

I have ideas, but I am unable to express it

I have lots to say. I have things I would like to verbalise, but it never comes out . It is so weird

Small rant done, but I wanna focus on casette futurism. I keep seeing it on my Reddit feed, and I want to understand why it fascinates me so much.

Compared to my wife, who is a designer, I am the stooge that has to really think why a particular design attracts me. And least to say, not that many

I am the kind of guy who dislikes minimalism, but maximalism. But even then, as per advised by my wife, even that takes effort since it will look like a junkyard than an art installment and/or expression of your soul if not done right. And yes, I know nothing even when I copy the damn design. t's like I can follow one-to-one IKEA's design that somehow speaks to me, but I won't like the end result.

But casette futurism somehow speaks to me. I like Apple's philosophy. I like Dieter Ram, and I do love Braun's design. Not a brutalist fan, more like the colonial design that I see in Samad Said Building or the old KTM building. They somehow excite and interest me.

Hell, even the first Ninjago City that I had bought really spoke to my soul. I bought it when I was at my lowest, and that really helped me move on. SO yeah, city design like that or Kowloon City mixed with the casette aesthetic really appeals to me.
