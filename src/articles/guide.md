## 2. Linguistic Upgrades
*   **Instead of "It works":** Use "Ensures predictable behavior under load."
*   **Instead of "I used [Tool]":** Use "Leveraged [Tool] to enforce separation of concerns and reduce technical debt."
*   **Instead of "I think we should...":** Use "Based on [Constraint X], I recommend..."
*   **Crucial Note:** Use these to sharpen your thinking, but always let the audience and context dictate the final language. Clarity trumps cleverness.

## 3. The "Senior-rify" Writing Framework
Filter your technical writing through these four pillars:

### Pillar 1: The Constraint (The "Why")
Define the boundaries: performance, security, time, or team context.
*   *Key Phrase:* "Given our high-concurrency requirements, we must prioritize..."

### Pillar 2: The Decision Matrix (The "How" & "Why Not")
Show the road not taken. Why was this solution superior, and what was explicitly rejected?
*   *Key Phrase:* "While [Option B] is faster to implement, [Option A] was chosen for long-term maintainability. We rejected [Option C] due to its lack of observability tooling."

### Pillar 3: The Long-Term Vision (The "What's Next")
Account for "Day 2" operations and future evolution.
*   *Key Phrase:* "This API is versioned and decoupled to allow for future integration with the analytics pipeline without breaking existing contracts."

### Pillar 4: The Measurable Impact (The "So What?")
Quantify the result. How did this decision improve the business, the product, or the team?
*   *Key Phrase:* "This optimization reduced database load by 20% during peak hours, deferring a costly infrastructure upgrade."
